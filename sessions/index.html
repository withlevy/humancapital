About This Event

Addressing the disconnect
in DC’s workforce ecosystem.

You’re doing real, meaningful work—and still, the people who need it most don’t always find it. Awareness is hard. Engagement is harder. Supporting workers in building relevant, durable skills is more difficult than ever.

Different parts of the ecosystem have figured out different pieces: tech meetup groups draw large crowds, libraries meet people where they are with minimal barriers, and adult education programs offer holistic, wraparound support.

What if we got everyone doing the work into the same room?

Let's work together to reach
the people who need us.

The Human Capital Sessions is a consistent, hands-on convening where DC’s workforce, education, tech, and community organizations come together to tackle a shared problem: how to reach, engage, and support the residents we all serve, more effectively, and with each other.

This is a collaborative working session where practitioners compare notes, test ideas, and build practical approaches they can take back to their programs. Real challenges. Real problem-solving. Real improvement.

Four times a year at MLK Library: a practitioner session and a public forum, back to back. We’re piloting it this April, and we’re inviting you to help shape it.

The State of Human Capital in the Capital

Opening Briefing

A short, shared grounding to start together. What residents are asking for. How the local economy is shifting. What the landscape looks like heading into Q2. Everyone starts from the same picture.

Opening
1:15 PM

"How I Solved This"

Lightning Rounds

Fast, practical knowledge-sharing. You're invited—never required—to take one minute and share a real engagement challenge you faced and what you did about it. No polish necessary. Just something honest and useful.

If you'd like to share, bring one slide:

  • ProblemOne sentence on what you were up against
  • SolutionTwo or three sentences on what you tried
  • ContactYour name and how to reach you

Working the Problem Together

Peer Consulting

Small groups. A shared case. Real challenges, across organizations that don't usually sit in the same room. Leave with at least one approach you hadn't considered—and at least one person you want to stay in touch with.
